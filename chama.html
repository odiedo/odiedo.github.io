Table Banking

Table banking is a community-based microfinance model where members pool their savings and lend to each other. Here are some rules for users:

  1. Membership: Users must be members of the table banking group to participate. Membership may require regular contributions to the group's savings pool.
  2. Savings: Each member contributes a predetermined amount regularly into the group's savings account. These savings serve as the capital base for lending activities.
  3. Loans: Members can apply for loans from the pooled savings. Loan applications are subject to approval by the group, based on factors such as repayment history, need, and available funds.
  4. Interest: Interest rates on loans are determined by the group and may vary based on factors such as loan term and purpose. The interest earned is redistributed among members as dividends or used to cover administrative expenses.
  5. Repayment: Borrowers are required to repay their loans according to the agreed-upon terms, which typically include a specified repayment period and schedule. Failure to repay may result in penalties or restrictions on future borrowing.
  6. Accountability: Transparency and accountability are essential. Regular meetings are held to review financial performance, discuss loan applications, and make collective decisions regarding the group's operations.
  7. Governance: The group may elect leaders or committees responsible for managing day-to-day operations, overseeing finances, and enforcing rules and policies.
  8. Risk Management: Measures are in place to mitigate risks, such as default by borrowers or mismanagement of funds. These may include loan guarantees, insurance, or savings reserves.

By adhering to these rules, users can benefit from access to affordable credit, opportunities for savings, and mutual support within the community. Table banking promotes financial inclusion and empowerment at the grassroots level.
